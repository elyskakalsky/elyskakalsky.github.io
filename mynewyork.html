My New York City

When I think of New York City the first thing that comes to my mind is the skyline, the bright lights, and the tourist attractions. People always dream of visiting, to get the true feeling of New York City. But the city holds more than just the skyline and lights, it has a Subway System that holds the entirety of New York City’s Spirit. The Subway System isn't just a way of traveling but it's a symbol of what New York City truly is, everyone moving fast focusing on their goals, the diversity in the city, and the experiences it brings to us. Every train station, and every passenger tells a different story about the city’s energy and how the people from the city just keep it running. The entire subway system connects all 5 of the boroughs and millions of lives, which makes it one of the strongest symbols of New York City’s identity.

Every ride I take on the subway reminds me how alive the city truly is, how people are always in motion. Every day there's millions of people riding trains like the 6 train or the Q train, traveling to work, school, and home. There's a constant movement showing how the city truly never stops. The early mornings are my favorite though. The air smells like coffee or a freshly toasted bagel, and the platforms buzzing with quiet determination. Business workers staring at their phones, or students holding tightly onto their backpacks. There's always someone going somewhere, no matter what time it is during the day. Even late at night, the train is still moving underground while the city above is still full of its glow. Watching the doors open and close at large stations like Times Square 42st feels like just watching the heart of the city itself. The movement of everyone and everything mirrors the determination of New Yorkers, people who are always pushing forward no matter how crowded or chaotic life gets.

Another connection the subway has to the city and the movement of New Yorkers is how it symbolizes how New York itself never sleeps. When most cities would be silent, New York City’s underground world is still full of life. The trains are always running 24 hours a day. I've even been on trains at 2 am, surrounded by sleepy workers heading home. You can see them at subway platforms such as restaurant workers heading home from long shifts, friends laughing coming home from concerts, or even night shift nurses on their way to the hospitals. The lights still flickering, the tunnels still echoing, and there's still a strange comfort knowing the city truly never sleeps. Stations like Union Square or Herald Square are alive throughout the entire night to even the early morning, street musicians with their endless energy performing till the very end no matter the time. This constant motion reflects on the unstoppable energy the city has. The subway proves how no matter the time in the day, there's always something happening somewhere in New York. People always have something to do pushing forward everyday or anytime.

Besides the subway being a symbol of the movement and energy of the city and New Yorkers, it also has a way of connecting people from every background or any neighborhood. Every time I step onto the train car, I'm stepping into a small world of stories. I've sat on one train cart where I saw a wall street worker reading a newspaper with a full suit, but on another I saw a full family visiting from another country just tryna figure out how to get around the place. You got students going from school to home, or even street musicians playing just to make a few bucks. You never know who you might find on the train. You also have places like Jackson Heights or Flushing, trains filled with people speaking in all sorts of languages, from Spanish to Mandarin to Bengali. Even though everyone comes from different backgrounds and different neighborhoods, for even a few stops on the train they are able to share the same space or even share the same destination. The mix of many different voices to many different stories is what makes the subway and the city such a unique place. It shows how New York City brings people together and is able to remind us that diversity is what gives the city its strength.

To me, the subway system is part of New York City’s identity, and is a part of mine too. The sound of “Stand clear of the closing doors, please” feels like the city’s voice. The art on the walls of stations all have a different meaning to them, telling a different story about a city that's creative and determined. When taking the 6th train for the last time and remembering the hidden city hall station, truly remembering how many different generations of people have taken these same trains before. The subway has seen the city grow, change and survive through many difficulties like blackouts, storms, and even pandemics, but it always has a way of coming back. It feels like a living symbol of New York City’s strength and resilience.

In the end, the subway is a perfect symbol of New York City because of how it captures everything the city stands for the movement, the diversity, and the endurance, It's where strangers share spaces, where the city keeps on moving no matter what and where the energy of millions of peoples comes together in one place. It's easy to say why everyone around the world wants to come to New York City and first thing: ride the train and say now I feel like a new yorker, stuck in the middle of the rushing crowd of Times Square, sharing a destination they wanna go to. People truly call the subway more than just a transportation, it's the heartbeat of New York City itself.
